About

Interdisciplinary researcher at the interface of computational modelling, large-scale imaging, and biomedical science.

My PhD focuses on multiscale reconstruction of neural circuits, combining synchrotron imaging (HiP-CT at ESRF), graph-based methods, and high-performance computing to model structure and organisation in human brain tissue. This work sits within the NIH CONNECTS Centre for Large-Scale Imaging of Neural Circuits.

I hold a medical degree from the University of Dundee (currently on research sabbatical) and an iBSc in Medical Physics and Biomedical Engineering from UCL, graduating with First Class Honours and a Dean's List Prize. My experience spans mechanistic and data-driven approaches, with applications in connectomics, developmental biology, and translational medical imaging.

Multi-Scale Connectomics in Terabyte-Scale Synchrotron Imaging

Applying cutting-edge synchrotron imaging (HiP-CT) and graph theory to scale up mapping of neural networks within the NIH CONNECTS Centre. Developing the BRIDGE neuroimaging pipeline — open-source, GPU-accelerated tools for reconstructing and analysing fibre networks across spatial scales.
